Post-Construction Cleaning Tips for Dunwoody New Builds

After the builders leave, a new build in Dunwoody requires more than just a quick sweep to be move-in ready. Post-construction cleaning is crucial for removing debris, dust, and residues, ensuring your new home is safe and habitable. Here’s a comprehensive guide tailored for Dunwoody residents to achieve a spotless finish.

Preparation Steps

  • Assess the Site: Identify debris, trip hazards, and special cleaning areas like kitchens or bathrooms. Document issues and ensure access, addressing any hazards like exposed wiring.
  • Gather Tools and PPE: Use HEPA vacuums, microfiber cloths, non-abrasive cleaners, plastic putty knives, and damp sponges. Wear gloves and high-reach tools; test cleaners on hidden spots first.
  • Remove Large Debris First: Clear nails, screws, wood scraps, insulation, tile pieces, and trash. Contractors often handle most, but check closets, corners, and exteriors like yards or garages.

Cleaning Process: Top-to-Bottom Approach

Work from ceilings downward to avoid re-contaminating cleaned areas.

  • Ceilings, Walls, Vents, and Lights: Dust and vacuum ceilings, pipes, beams, fans, grates, and ducts with HEPA vacuum; wipe walls, trim, doors, baseboards, moldings, and hardware with dry dusting first, then non-abrasive agents.
  • Windows, Blinds, and Frames: Dust interiors, sills, and tracks; clean glass with cleaner, removing fingerprints and debris.
  • Floors and Tiles: Sweep/vacuum all surfaces (including upholstery), then mop/disinfect; for tiles, use a damp sponge, dry towel, putty knife for residue, and warm damp towel to soften stubborn spots before buffing.
  • Kitchens and Bathrooms: Thoroughly wipe/sanitize cabinets (inside/out), counters, appliances, sinks, fixtures, mirrors, knobs, drains, and grout; remove grout/paint drips.
  • Other Areas: Vacuum carpets deeply (hot water extraction if needed), clean upholstery, hardware, shelves, and air vents/ducts; seal closets during the process.

Air Quality and Final Tips

  • Use HEPA Vacuums: Daily during cleanup and high-quality air filters; vacuum vents regularly.
  • Exterior Cleaning: Sweep walkways/driveways, pressure wash walls/patios/fences/gutters, and clear yard debris.
  • Professional Help: For large new builds, consider hiring professionals if DIY feels overwhelming, especially for shine-level results.

Conclusion

Following this checklist will ensure thoroughness, adapting to your home's layout and materials. Post-construction cleaning is a detailed process that guarantees your new Dunwoody home is pristine and ready for you to move in. Whether you choose to tackle this task yourself or enlist professional services, a clean start in your new home is invaluable.
