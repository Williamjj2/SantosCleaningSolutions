Seasonal Cleaning: Preparing Your Johns Creek Home for Spring

As the weather warms up and the days grow longer, it's time to give your Johns Creek home a thorough spring cleaning. This comprehensive checklist will help you tackle the pollen and allergens common in Georgia, refresh your indoor spaces, and prepare your outdoor areas for the season. Whether you plan to do it yourself or hire local professionals, these tips will ensure your home is spotless and ready for spring.

Preparation Tips

  • Declutter: Go room by room to donate unused items, simplifying your cleaning process.
  • Eco-Friendly Cleaners: Use natural products like vinegar, baking soda, and lemon for safe cleaning around pets and kids.
  • Ventilation: Open windows for fresh air and reverse ceiling fan directions to improve airflow.
  • Outdoor Maintenance: Pressure wash patios, walkways, and siding to remove pollen and mildew, and clean outdoor furniture.

Kitchen

  • Vacuum under the fridge and clean coils; remove items from counters and sanitize surfaces, including the backsplash.
  • Deep clean the dishwasher, sink, drain, and faucet using dish soap and a toothbrush.
  • Wipe down cabinets, appliances, light fixtures, switches, and baseboards; mop the floors.

Bathrooms

  • Empty and wipe down cabinets and drawers; organize items and check expiration dates on medicines and makeup.
  • Sanitize counters, sinks, and mirrors; clean the tub, shower, and toilet, including hinges and bolts.
  • Reseal grout if necessary; wipe doors, knobs, and baseboards; vacuum and mop floors.

Bedrooms & Living Areas

  • Launder curtains, pillows, blankets, and duvets; flip and air out mattresses, sprinkle with baking soda, and vacuum.
  • Dust furniture, decor, electronics, light fixtures, and ceiling fans; clean blinds, windows, and sills.
  • Organize closets and drawers, switch seasonal clothes; vacuum/shampoo carpets, mop hard floors, and wash rugs.

Whole Home

Area Key Tasks
Floors/Walls Vacuum/shampoo carpets, mop hard floors, wash rugs; dust/wash baseboards, walls, and corners.
Windows/Doors Wash windows/screens/sills, clean blinds; wipe doors, knobs, trims, and switches.
Vents/Overlooked Wash vent covers, vacuum vents; clean behind appliances.
Outdoor (Johns Creek-specific) Pressure wash patios/siding for pollen/mildew; stage furniture for outdoor enjoyment.

Work room-by-room over several days, or hire professionals for tasks like carpet shampooing or pressure washing. This routine will improve indoor air quality and prepare your home for the vibrant spring season.

Conclusion

A well-executed spring cleaning can transform your home, improve your family's health, and make your living space more inviting. By following this guide, Johns Creek residents can ensure their homes are fresh, clean, and ready to embrace the new season.
